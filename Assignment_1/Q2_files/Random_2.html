Hariton Peak (Bulgarian: връх Харитон, ‘Vrah Hariton’ \'vr&h ha-ri-'ton\) is the rocky peak rising to 1880 m near the north end of Sentinel Range in Ellsworth Mountains, Antarctica. It is named after Pop (Priest) Hariton (Hariton Halachev, 1835-1876), a leader of the 1876 April Uprising for Bulgarian independence.

Location[edit]

Hariton Peak is located at 77°26′37″S 86°18′27″W﻿ / ﻿77.44361°S 86.30750°W﻿ / -77.44361; -86.30750Coordinates: 77°26′37″S 86°18′27″W﻿ / ﻿77.44361°S 86.30750°W﻿ / -77.44361; -86.30750, which is 1.68 km northeast of Mount Lymburner, 6.6 km south of Mount Liavaag and 8.25 km west-northwest of Mount Weems. US mapping in 1961.
